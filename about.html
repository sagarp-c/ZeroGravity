About PACE Explorer

The PACE Explorer platform is designed to bring NASA’s cutting-edge PACE (Plankton, Aerosol, Cloud, ocean Ecosystem) satellite data to classrooms around the world. Our mission is to help students and educators understand the important role that Earth’s oceans and atmosphere play in the planet's climate system. By offering interactive data visualizations, engaging educational videos, and lesson plans, we aim to improve ocean literacy and inspire the next generation of scientists and environmentalists.

Through real-time data visualizations of ocean and atmospheric health, we make NASA’s vast, open-source data accessible and easy to understand. From tracking phytoplankton blooms to exploring the carbon cycle, PACE Explorer helps students engage with real-world data and understand how the ocean and atmosphere interact to regulate Earth’s climate.

What is the PACE Mission?

The Plankton, Aerosol, Cloud, ocean Ecosystem (PACE) mission, launched in February 2024, is one of NASA’s most ambitious efforts to monitor the Earth’s oceans and atmosphere. By using cutting-edge instruments, PACE helps scientists understand how:

  • Phytoplankton (tiny ocean plants) contribute to marine ecosystems and the global carbon cycle.
  • The carbon exchange between the ocean and the atmosphere affects Earth’s climate.
  • Aerosols influence the growth of phytoplankton and impact cloud formation.

Instruments on PACE:

  • OCI (Ocean Color Instrument): Measures light reflected by the ocean to detect phytoplankton and other important marine indicators.
  • SPEXone Polarimeter and HARP2 Polarimeter: Measure aerosols and cloud properties, helping scientists understand how these factors influence the Earth’s atmosphere and oceans.

Our Goal

We created PACE Explorer to provide a bridge between the complex data gathered by NASA’s PACE mission and the classroom. Our platform offers:

  • Real-time visualizations of global ocean and atmospheric data.
  • Lesson plans designed by educators, aligned with the Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS).
  • Games and interactive tools that bring ocean and atmosphere science to life.
